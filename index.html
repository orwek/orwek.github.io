JavaScript

My passion for JavaScript probably stems from owning a low end smartphone. After being denied the third or fourth time I tried to install a certain app because of the limited memory, I started writing web apps that could run on smart phones. The first of these was a simple dice rolling app. From there I started writing games in JavaScript, and the rest as they say is history.

The thing I love the most about writing games is that it gives me the opportunity to explore parts of a programming language that I don't normally get to use in day to day programming. Much of what I learn can then be brought back to work because I have delved into an area that I normally wouldn't have touched and it makes me a better programmer.
